After looking at the three xkcd comics, my favorite was “Lakes and Oceans.” I liked this one the most because it took something that could have been boring or hard to understand and made it really interesting. Instead of only showing numbers, it uses visual comparisons to help the reader actually imagine how deep certain lakes and oceans are. That made the information feel more real to me, not just like random facts on a page.

What I found most engaging was how surprising some of the comparisons were. I did not realize how extreme the depth differences were until I saw them shown visually. The comic also has a creative and slightly funny style, which makes it more memorable. It feels less like a science chart and more like something that invites you to stop, look closely, and think. I think that is part of what makes it so effective.

I also think the format was a smart choice. Since the main idea is about comparing depth and scale, the visual layout helps the reader understand the information much faster than a paragraph or regular graph would. You can immediately see the differences, and that makes the message stronger. Out of the three comics, this one told the most engaging story to me because it was clear, creative, and easy to connect with. It made me curious, and it showed that data can be both informative and fun at the same time.
